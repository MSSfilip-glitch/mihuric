# Diving at DC Mihurić

### Organizing a diving vacation in Selce, Croatia

You might be wondering where to dive in Croatia. Well, you came to the right place. Along with beginner, advanced and professional PADI diving courses (link to DSD, PADI courses, PADI Pro), our diving center offers **shore dives, night dives and boat dives** at various diving locations. You can dive with your buddy/group or one of our staff members can be your guide.

## **Shore dives**

A beautiful house reef awaits you **right in front of our diving center**. It doesn’t get any more convenient than this. Load up your diving gear, take a few steps and you’re there. **Take a look for yourself** if you don’t believe us (link to live webcam). Shore dives are possible throughout the day, but we also organize night dives multiple times per week.

## **Night dives**

Night dives are your chance to discover the underwater world after sundown. Night dives are organized **a few times per week**, mostly on the house reef. Every so often we do a night dive at another location that can be reached by boat.

## **Boat dives**

With our **speedboat Triton**, various interesting diving locations are reached in no time. We organize dives on **30 diving locations** (link to list of diving locations). The boat goes **2 times a day**, in the morning and in the afternoon. The location is decided in advance, but we also take divers’ suggestions and weather conditions into consideration. Locations include shipwrecks, reefs, caves…

## **When should I visit?**

The mild climate (link to Weather and climate) ensures a comfortable vacation any time of year. **Summer** is the busiest part of the year, not only for us, but for the rest of the town. So, in July and August you can expect crowded beaches and warm, sunny weather. If you prefer a slightly calmer atmosphere, but still enjoy toasty temperatures, we recommend **September**, when weather-wise, it’s still full on summer here. In **October** the temperatures tend to drop a bit, but we usually still get a lot of sunny days. In the **early spring** the water hasn’t quite warmed up yet, but some make their first dives of the year then, while others follow in May.
